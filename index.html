Victoria on the Move

Victoria has a road network of around 23,000km with a value of $27 billion, it supports 350 million tonnes of freight each year. The road network is crutial to keeping victoria moving and allowing the economy to thrive.

Safety and efficiency are at the core of the road network and Victorians demand the highest level of safety possible.
This analysis will provide a general overview into accidents that occurred in 2018 as well as an in-depth look into one of the projects that has improved safety on Victoria's roads. The data was obtained from the Victorian Department of Transportation and can be accessed via the link at the bottom of this webpage.

The map of victoria above shows the distribution of accidents that occurred during 2018, one dot represents one accident. As you can see there is a high density of collisison around areas with higher populations. This issue will be visited in the next section of the report.

Weekends and Weekdays

The bar chart on the left shows the number of accidents by day of the week and also allows you to select an accident type. Breaking down accidents by the day of the week produces some interesting insights. There is a distinct difference in common accidents on weekends compared to weekdays, for example there is significantly more collisions with other cars on the weekdays likely due to increased traffic loads. In contrast, there is far more accidents that don't involve a collision such as spinning out of control that occur on weekends which is likely due to people driving in less familiar places on weekend outings.
